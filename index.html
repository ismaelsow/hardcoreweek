Got questions?

How long do I have access to the program and its material?

After the live classes, you get permanent access to all of the bootcamp's resources and material: recordings, lessons, exercises with solutions, extra-resources etc.

What flavor of SQL will I learn?

We'll be using the Google Standard SQL dialect on the Google BigQuery database. It shouldn't matter much as the syntax variations that exist between database engines (Snowflake, Oracle, PostgreSQL, MySQL etc.) are small and easy to pick up.

Does the program cover database design, modification or administration topics?

No. The bootcamp focuses on analysis and reporting, i.e answering business questions with SELECT-based queries. We cover user-defined functions, table functions, and views as they can be used to make better SELECT-based queries. We don't include anything that create, update or delete database records.

What equipment and software do I need?

Many teaching programs, courses and books ask you to install some desktop database software and toy datasets. We don't. There are too many horror stories of students unable to install the software and staying stuck. For the database, we use Google BigQuery. The live classes are taught over Google Meet. Both BigQuery and Meet have free plans and are browser-based. Bottom line: you only need a computer with an internet connection, and a Gmail account.

What is expected from me during the program?

To get the most out of the bootcamp, you should actively attend the live classes and do the practice exercises. Asking and answering questions on the community Discord is also a good idea.

I have a full-time job. Will it work for me?

We know that life is busy. That's why the live classes are done during the weekend. Then you can consolidate your skills whenever you have time, using the recorded lessons and exercises.

What will be the outcome of the bootcamp?

After completing the program, you'll be comfortable reading and writing advanced SQL queries for any of the following roles: data analyst, data engineer, analytics engineer, BI analyst, business analyst, web analyst, etc.

How does the coaching call work?
