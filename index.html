Got questions?

How long do I have access to the program and its material?

The Hardcore Week of SQL is a permanent bootcamp, that means that when you join, you get permanent access to the live classes and all of the bootcamp's resources and material.

What flavor of SQL will I learn?

We'll be using the Google Standard SQL dialect on the Google BigQuery database. It shouldn't matter much as the small syntax variations that exist between database engines (Snowflake, Oracle, PostgreSQL, MySQL etc.) are easy to pick up.

Does the program cover database design, modification or administration topics?

No. The bootcamp focuses on analysis and reporting, i.e answering business questions with SELECT-based queries. We cover user-defined functions, table functions and stored procedures as they can be used to make better SELECT-based queries. But we don't include anything that create, update or delete database records.

What equipment and software do I need?

Many teaching programs, courses and books ask you to install some desktop database software and toy datasets. We don't. There are too many horror stories of students unable to install the software and staying stuck. We use the Google BigQuery console which is accessible with any web browser. Basically you only need a computer with an internet connection.

What is expected from me during the program?

To get the most out of the bootcamp, you should actively attend the live classes and do the practice exercises as well as the final projects. Asking and answering questions on the community Discord is also a good idea.

I have a full-time job. Can I attend the training?

We know that life is busy. That's why we created a permanent bootcamp. That means that you can hop in a live class whenever you have time, and take as much time as you need to complete the program. You could be done in a week if you can take a week off. Or you could take it more slowly and finish in six months or in a year. Also, you can always work asynchronously using the recorded lessons and exercises.

What will be the outcome of the bootcamp?

After completing the program, you'll be comfortable reading and writing advanced SQL queries for any of the following roles: data analyst, data engineer, analytics engineer, BI analyst, business analyst, web analyst, etc.
